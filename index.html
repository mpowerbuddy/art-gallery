Dune Study #1

Materials: Blue ballpoint pen on aged book page

The horizon is a promise of continuity. I wanted to suggest vastness through restraint—fewer lines, more implication. The wind striations become denser as they approach the horizon, inverting our expectation of detail. What draws the eye is what isn't there.

Monolith Void

Materials: Fine liner (0.3mm) on blank sheet

Form through absence. The void inside the monolith is more present than its edges—by defining boundaries without filling space, the object gains weight. The tiny human silhouette reminds us scale. We are small against what we build.

Jazz Sketch

Materials: Red ballpoint on scrap score paper

Improvisational notation. The primary melody arc struggles upward while the bass walks steadily below. Rest marks represent what isn't played—silence anchors rhythm. This isn't a transcription; it's a feeling of the form.

Desert Breath

Materials: Extra-fine Sharpie on large format

Minimalism pushed to discomfort. Everything here is almost nothing. The single stone, its shadow, the whisper of atmosphere. I wanted the viewer to feel the emptiness—to breathe with the composition. Whitespace is active, not passive.
