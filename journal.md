## Techniques to Master

### Crosshatching Mastery
- Diagonal layers at 45°, 60°, 90°
- Overlap counts = tonal value
- Leave gaps for midtones
- Perpendicular strokes for darkest values

### Stroke Economy
- One continuous line when possible
- Lift = emphasis, not failure
- Plan path before drawing
- Let the pen breathe

### Organic Irregularity
- Lines are never perfectly straight
- Hand pressure varies naturally
- Embrace the "mistake" as character
- Nature has no parallel lines forever

### Plotter Optimization
- Minimize pen lifts (combine paths)
- Stroke order: dark to light
- Consider multiple passes for density
- Paper texture affects ink spread

## Technique Notes — What I Learned Today

### Crosshatching Direction
- Don't hatch parallel to the form—hatch ACROSS to show volume
- Vary stroke length for texture variation
- Leave intentional gaps for midtones

### Continuous Line
- One path = one breath
- Lift only for emphasis
- The hand knows more than the mind

### Irregularity
- No straight lines exist in nature
- Slight wobble = character
- Perfect = dead

### Plotter Considerations
- Longer paths = fewer lifts
- Same-direction strokes = less pen travel
- Dense areas need path planning
